Text-Based Questions

  1. Start with a clear and concise question that is easy to understand.
  2. Choose a format that works well with the content being discussed, such as fill-in-the-blank, short answer, or open-ended response.
  3. Use appropriate grammar and spelling to ensure clarity and understanding.
  4. Provide context or additional information when necessary to help the reader better understand the question.
  5. Keep the language simple and avoid using technical jargon or complex vocabulary, unless it is essential to the question.

Multiple-Choice Questions

  1. Start with a clear and concise question stem that is easy to understand.
  2. Keep the options consistent in length and structure to reduce confusion.
  3. Avoid using negatives in your question or answer choices, as these can be confusing.
  4. Use plausible distractors that will challenge the learner and accurately assess their knowledge of the topic.
  5. Clearly indicate how many answer choices the learner is expected to select, such as "Select all that apply" or "Choose two options."
